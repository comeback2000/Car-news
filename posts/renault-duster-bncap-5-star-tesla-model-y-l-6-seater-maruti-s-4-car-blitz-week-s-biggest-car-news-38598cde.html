Renault Duster BNCAP 5-Star, Tesla Model Y L 6-Seater, Maruti's 4-Car Blitz: Week's Biggest Car News

The past week in the Indian automotive sector was packed with significant developments, from a landmark safety rating for a popular SUV to the arrival of a new six-seat electric crossover and a flurry of launch plans from the country's largest carmaker. Here's a detailed look at the stories that defined the week and what they mean for car buyers and the industry.

Renault Duster Achieves 5-Star BNCAP Rating

Renault's iconic Duster has become the latest SUV to be tested under the Bharat New Car Assessment Programme (BNCAP), and it has emerged with a perfect five-star safety rating for adult occupant protection. This makes the Duster the first Renault model to be evaluated under India's own crash-test regime, and it joins a growing list of mass-market SUVs that have proven their structural integrity.

The Duster scored full marks across major safety parameters, including frontal offset, side impact, and pole side impact tests. While the model has long been appreciated for its ruggedness and off-road capability, this official validation is expected to significantly boost buyer confidence. Safety has become a key purchase criterion in India, especially after BNCAP ratings began influencing consumer decisions. The Duster's result puts it on par with competitors like the Mahindra Scorpio-N and Tata Harrier, which have also achieved five-star scores.

For Renault India, this is a crucial marketing win. The Duster, which was relaunched in 2024 after a hiatus, now has a safety credential that can sway families and fleet buyers. The test results also underscore the importance of BNCAP in pushing manufacturers to improve structural safety across price segments.

Tesla Model Y L: 6-Seater Electric SUV Launched in India

Tesla has expanded its India lineup with the launch of the Model Y L, a stretched, three-row variant of its best-selling electric SUV. The Model Y L features a longer body and wheelbase compared to the standard Model Y, enabling a 2+2+2 seating layout that accommodates six passengers. This is a direct response to the growing demand for family-oriented electric vehicles with flexible seating.

The Model Y L is available in a single 250 Plus variant, powered by a rear-wheel drivetrain and a single battery pack that delivers a claimed driving range of over 750 km on a single charge. For those needing all-wheel drive, Tesla also offers a dual-motor AWD version with a range of over 500 km. The pricing starts at approximately ₹65 lakh (ex-showroom), positioning it against the Mercedes-Benz EQB and the upcoming Kia EV9.

This launch is significant for two reasons. First, it brings a six-seat electric option to India, a segment that is currently underserved. Second, it demonstrates Tesla's commitment to the Indian market despite import duties and infrastructure challenges. The Model Y L is imported as a completely built unit (CBU), but Tesla has indicated plans to localise production if volumes justify it.

Maruti Suzuki's Four-Car Launch Blitz by 2027

Maruti Suzuki is preparing for one of its busiest product launch cycles in recent memory. Over the next 12 to 18 months, the company will introduce four new models: a Brezza facelift, the YMC electric SUV, and updates to the Fronx and Baleno.

Brezza Facelift

The Brezza, Maruti's compact SUV, will receive a mid-cycle facelift with a redesigned front grille, new alloy wheels, and updated LED lighting. Inside, the biggest change is a 10.1-inch touchscreen infotainment system, replacing the current 9-inch unit, along with improved connected car features and a digital instrument cluster. The facelift is expected to launch by mid-2027.

YMC EV: Maruti's First Electric SUV

The YMC (Young Mind Concept) EV is Maruti's first dedicated electric SUV, built on a new skateboard platform. It is expected to launch by late 2026 or early 2027, with two battery pack options: a 40 kWh unit for a range of around 350 km and a 60 kWh unit for over 500 km. The YMC will be positioned as a premium compact SUV, competing with the Tata Curvv EV and MG ZS EV.

Fronx and Baleno Updates

The Fronx coupe-SUV and the Baleno hatchback will receive feature updates, including the same 10.1-inch screen, updated ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems), and new colour options. These updates are aimed at keeping the models fresh against rivals like the Hyundai i20 and Kia Sonet.

Maruti's aggressive launch schedule reflects its strategy to defend its market share against rising competition from Hyundai, Kia, and Tata, while also transitioning to electric mobility. The YMC EV, in particular, will be a litmus test for Maruti's EV ambitions.

Mercedes-Benz CLA Electric: Entry-Level Luxury EV Arrives

Mercedes-Benz launched the all-electric CLA in India, marking the brand's entry-level electric sedan. The CLA Electric is available in a single 250 Plus variant, featuring a rear-wheel drivetrain and a claimed range of over 750 km (WLTP cycle). The sedan is priced at around ₹55 lakh (ex-showroom), making it the most affordable electric Mercedes in the country.

The CLA Electric is built on the Mercedes-Benz Modular Architecture (MMA) and features an 800-volt architecture for ultra-fast charging. It can add up to 300 km of range in just 15 minutes using a DC fast charger. The interior is equipped with the MBUX Superscreen, a 10.25-inch digital instrument cluster, and a 14-inch central touchscreen.

This launch is aimed at attracting premium sedan buyers who are considering the Tesla Model 3 or the BMW i4. With its long range and fast charging, the CLA Electric could become a strong contender in the luxury EV space.

JSW Motors Plans Experience Centres Ahead of Debut

JSW Motors, the automotive arm of the JSW Group, has announced plans to set up at least four experience centres in Mumbai, New Delhi, and Ahmedabad before launching its first car in the second half of 2026. The company is hoping to create a premium retail experience similar to what Tesla and BYD offer.

JSW's debut product is expected to be an electric SUV, developed in partnership with a Chinese OEM. The experience centres will showcase the brand's technology and allow customers to test drive vehicles before booking. This move signals JSW's serious intent to compete in India's EV market, which is expected to grow rapidly over the next decade.

Honda to Exit South Korea Car Market; India Unaffected

Honda has announced it will stop selling cars in South Korea by the end of 2026, citing poor sales and intense competition from local brands like Hyundai and Kia. However, the company will continue to provide after-sales service and parts support for existing customers. Honda will also focus on its motorcycle business in South Korea, where it has a stronger presence.

For Indian consumers, this decision has no direct impact. Honda Cars India continues to operate independently, with models like the City, Amaze, and Elevate. However, it highlights the challenges foreign automakers face in markets dominated by strong local players. Honda's India strategy remains focused on hybrid and CNG powertrains, with an electric SUV expected by 2028.

What This Means for Car Buyers

  • Safety First: The Duster's 5-star BNCAP rating gives buyers another affordable, safe SUV option. Always check BNCAP ratings before purchasing.
  • 6-Seater EV Choice: The Tesla Model Y L is the first six-seat electric SUV in India, ideal for families wanting zero-emission mobility with flexible seating.
  • Maruti's EV Entry: The YMC EV will bring Maruti's reliability and service network to the EV space, potentially making electric cars more accessible.
  • Luxury EV Options: The Mercedes CLA Electric offers a compelling combination of range, charging speed, and brand prestige at a relatively accessible price.

Future Outlook

The next 12-18 months will be transformative for the Indian car market. With Maruti launching its first EV, Tesla expanding its lineup, and new players like JSW entering the fray, competition will intensify. Buyers can expect more choice, better safety features, and advanced technology across segments. The shift towards electric mobility is accelerating, but internal combustion engine models will remain relevant for years, especially in rural and semi-urban areas.

As BNCAP ratings become a standard reference, manufacturers will invest more in structural safety. The days of sub-4-meter SUVs with low safety scores are numbered. For consumers, this is a win-win: safer cars, more electric options, and better value.

Conclusion

From the Renault Duster's five-star safety triumph to the arrival of Tesla's six-seat Model Y L and Maruti's ambitious four-car plan, the past week has set the tone for an exciting year ahead. Whether you're in the market for a safe family SUV, a luxury electric sedan, or a feature-packed hatchback, there's plenty to look forward to.
