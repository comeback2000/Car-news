The past week in the Indian automotive industry was packed with major developments spanning safety ratings, new electric vehicle launches, and a landmark flex-fuel introduction. From the Renault Duster becoming the latest SUV to earn a full five-star Bharat NCAP rating to Tesla expanding its Model Y lineup with a six-seater variant, here is a comprehensive look at the stories that dominated headlines.

Renault Duster Achieves 5-Star BNCAP Safety Rating

Renault’s popular compact SUV, the Duster, has become the first model from the French automaker to be tested under the Bharat New Car Assessment Programme (BNCAP). The Duster scored a full five-star rating across all major safety parameters, placing it among the safest vehicles in its segment. This result is significant for Indian car buyers who increasingly prioritize safety, especially in the competitive compact SUV space where rivals like the Hyundai Creta and Kia Seltos have also performed well in global NCAP tests. The Duster’s strong showing is expected to boost consumer confidence and may influence purchasing decisions in the coming months.

Tesla Model Y L: 6-Seater Electric SUV Launched in India

Tesla has introduced a new variant of its best-selling Model Y in India: the Model Y L. This version features a longer body and extended wheelbase, enabling a 2+2+2 three-row seating layout. The Model Y L is offered with a dual-motor all-wheel-drive system and a claimed range of over 500 km on a single charge. This launch expands Tesla’s Indian lineup beyond the standard Model Y and Model 3, catering to families seeking an electric SUV with genuine third-row space. The Model Y L competes with the Mercedes EQB and upcoming electric SUVs from BMW and Volvo.

Mercedes-Benz CLA Electric Launched as Entry-Level EV

Mercedes-Benz has also entered the entry-level electric sedan segment with the launch of the CLA Electric. The EV is available in a single 250 Plus variant, featuring a rear-wheel-drive setup and a claimed driving range of over 750 km. This positions the CLA Electric as a compelling option for luxury EV buyers who prioritize range. The launch underscores the growing competition in the premium EV space, with Tesla and Mercedes now offering distinct choices for Indian consumers.

Maruti Suzuki Launches India's First Flex-Fuel Car

In a move aimed at reducing dependence on fossil fuels, Maruti Suzuki has launched India’s first flex-fuel car, the WagonR Flex Fuel. This model can run on E85 fuel (85% ethanol blended with 15% petrol), making it compatible with the newly introduced E85 fuel priced at Rs 82.12 per litre. The launch aligns with the government’s push for biofuels and energy security. Maruti’s flex-fuel technology allows existing petrol engines to run on high-ethanol blends with minimal modifications, offering a practical pathway to lower emissions without requiring a complete shift to electric powertrains.

Maruti Suzuki's Upcoming Product Blitz: Brezza Facelift, YMC EV, and More

Maruti Suzuki is preparing for one of its busiest launch phases in recent years. Over the next 12 to 18 months, the company is expected to introduce four new models: a Brezza facelift, the YMC electric SUV, and updates to the Fronx and Baleno. The Brezza facelift will feature a 10.1-inch touchscreen (up from the current 9-inch unit) and enhanced connected car features. The YMC EV, slated for a late 2026 or early 2027 launch, will be offered with two battery pack options, marking Maruti’s first dedicated electric vehicle. These launches are crucial for Maruti to maintain its market leadership amid increasing competition from Hyundai, Kia, and Tata Motors.

Other Notable Developments

Honda to Exit South Korea Car Market by End of 2026

Honda has announced it will cease car sales in South Korea by the end of 2026, though it will continue after-sales service and focus on its motorcycle business in the country. This strategic retreat does not affect Honda’s operations in India, where the company continues to sell models like the City and Amaze. However, it reflects the challenging global landscape for mid-sized automakers in markets dominated by local giants like Hyundai and Kia.

JSW Motors Plans Experience Centres Ahead of Debut

JSW Motors, the automotive arm of the JSW Group, is setting up at least four experience centres in Mumbai, New Delhi, and Ahmedabad before launching its first car in the second half of 2026. The company aims to create a premium brand experience, signaling its ambition to compete with established players. The debut product is expected to be an electric SUV, leveraging JSW’s partnership with MG Motor.

Analysis and Implications for Car Buyers

The past week’s news highlights several trends that directly impact Indian car buyers. Safety is becoming a non-negotiable feature, with BNCAP ratings influencing purchase decisions. The Renault Duster’s five-star score gives it a strong edge over competitors that have not yet been tested. In the EV space, the Tesla Model Y L and Mercedes CLA Electric offer distinct value propositions: Tesla focuses on space and all-wheel drive, while Mercedes emphasizes range and luxury. For budget-conscious buyers, Maruti’s flex-fuel WagonR provides an affordable alternative to full EVs, especially as E85 fuel becomes more widely available. Meanwhile, Maruti’s upcoming models promise more technology and electrification, ensuring the compact SUV and hatchback segments remain competitive.

Future Outlook

The next 12 to 18 months will be transformative for the Indian car market. With Maruti Suzuki launching its first EV, Tesla expanding its lineup, and new entrants like JSW Motors entering the fray, consumers will have more choices than ever. Safety regulations will continue to tighten, pushing more automakers to achieve high BNCAP scores. Flex-fuel technology could bridge the gap between petrol and electric, especially in rural areas where ethanol production is strong. However, the success of these initiatives will depend on infrastructure development, including charging stations and ethanol dispensing pumps.

Conclusion

From Renault’s safety milestone to Tesla’s family-friendly EV and Maruti’s flex-fuel innovation, the past week underscored the dynamic nature of India’s automotive industry. Car buyers stand to benefit from safer, more efficient, and more diverse options across price points.
