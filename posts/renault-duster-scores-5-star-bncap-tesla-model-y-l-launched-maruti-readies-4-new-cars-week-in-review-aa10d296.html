Renault Duster Scores 5-Star BNCAP, Tesla Model Y L Launched, Maruti Readies 4 New Cars: Week in Review

The past week has been eventful for the Indian automotive market, with a mix of safety accolades, new electric vehicle launches, and a glimpse into Maruti Suzuki's ambitious product pipeline. Leading the headlines is the Renault Duster's five-star Bharat NCAP score, followed by Tesla's introduction of a six-seater Model Y L in India and Mercedes-Benz's entry-level electric CLA. Meanwhile, Maruti Suzuki confirmed plans for four new models by 2027. Here's a detailed look at the key developments.

Renault Duster Achieves 5-Star BNCAP Safety Rating

The Renault Duster has become the latest SUV to be tested under Bharat NCAP (BNCAP) and has secured a full five-star safety rating for adult occupant protection. It is the first Renault model to undergo the Indian crash test program. The Duster scored well across frontal offset, side impact, and pole impact tests, reinforcing its structural integrity. This result places the Duster among the safest vehicles in its segment, alongside competitors like the Hyundai Creta and Mahindra Scorpio N, which have also achieved five-star ratings. For buyers, this is a strong endorsement of the Duster's safety credentials, especially as BNCAP ratings increasingly influence purchase decisions.

Tesla Model Y L: 6-Seater Electric SUV Launched in India

Tesla has expanded its India lineup with the Model Y L, a stretched version of the popular Model Y. The key differentiator is the third row of seats in a 2+2+2 configuration, making it a six-seater. The Model Y L features a longer wheelbase and body, providing additional passenger space. It is offered with a dual-motor all-wheel-drive system and a claimed range of over 500 km. This variant joins the existing Model Y lineup, which includes the 250 Plus variant with a rear-wheel-drive setup and a range of over 750 km. The Model Y L is priced competitively against other three-row electric SUVs in the market, such as the Kia EV9 and the upcoming Hyundai Ioniq 7.

Mercedes-Benz CLA Electric: Entry-Level EV Sedan Launched

Mercedes-Benz has introduced the CLA Electric as its most affordable electric sedan in India. The CLA Electric is available in a single 250 Plus variant, powered by a rear-wheel-drive system and a single battery pack that delivers a claimed driving range of over 750 km. This positions it as a strong contender in the luxury EV space, directly competing with the BMW i4 and the Audi Q6 e-tron. The CLA Electric features a sleek coupe-like design, a 10.1-inch touchscreen infotainment system, and Level 2 ADAS features. Mercedes-Benz aims to attract new-age luxury buyers with this model, offering a blend of style, range, and technology.

Maruti Suzuki's Four New Cars by 2027

Maruti Suzuki has outlined plans to launch four new models in India over the next 12 to 18 months. The lineup includes a Brezza facelift, the YMC EV (a new electric SUV), and updates to the Fronx and Baleno. The Brezza facelift is expected to get a 10.1-inch touchscreen (up from the current 9-inch unit), improved connected features, and revised styling. The YMC EV, likely to launch by late 2026 or early 2027, will be Maruti's second electric vehicle after the e Vitara, with two battery pack options. The Fronx and Baleno updates will focus on minor cosmetic tweaks and feature enhancements. This aggressive launch schedule underscores Maruti's strategy to maintain its market leadership amid growing competition from Hyundai, Kia, and Tata Motors.

Other Notable Developments

  • JSW Motors Experience Centres: JSW Motors plans to set up at least four experience centres in Mumbai, New Delhi, and Ahmedabad before launching its debut car in the second half of 2026. The company is betting on a direct-to-consumer model.
  • Honda Exits South Korea Car Market: Honda has announced it will stop selling cars in South Korea by end of 2026, though it will continue after-sales service and focus on motorcycles. The move does not affect Honda's India operations.

Analysis and Market Implications

The week's news highlights several trends. First, BNCAP ratings are becoming a critical differentiator for mass-market SUVs, pushing manufacturers to improve structural safety. Renault's five-star score for the Duster could boost its sales, especially among safety-conscious buyers. Second, the EV segment is heating up with Tesla and Mercedes-Benz launching models that offer long range and premium features, putting pressure on domestic players like Tata and Mahindra to accelerate their EV plans. Third, Maruti Suzuki's multi-pronged approach—refreshing existing models and introducing an EV—shows its intent to defend its market share across segments.

Future Outlook

The next few months will see more BNCAP results, including tests for the Hyundai Creta and Kia Seltos facelifts. Tesla is expected to expand its service network in India, while Maruti's YMC EV will be closely watched as a volume electric SUV. The entry of JSW Motors could disrupt the market with new retail formats. Overall, the Indian car market is becoming more safety-conscious, electrified, and competitive.

Conclusion

From Renault's safety milestone to Tesla's six-seater EV and Maruti's product blitz, the past week has set the tone for an exciting year ahead. Buyers have more choices than ever, and manufacturers are raising the bar on safety, range, and features. Stay tuned for more updates as these stories develop.
