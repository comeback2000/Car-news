Car News That Made Headlines Over The Past Week: Renault Duster’s BNCAP Performance, A 6-Seater Tesla And More

The past week in the Indian car market has been eventful, with a mix of safety milestones, new EV launches, and strategic announcements from major automakers. Renault’s Duster became the brand’s first Bharat NCAP-tested model, scoring a full five-star rating. Tesla expanded its Model Y lineup with a six-seater ‘L’ variant, while Mercedes-Benz introduced its entry-level electric sedan, the CLA. Maruti Suzuki outlined plans for four new models by 2027, and JSW Motors confirmed its entry into the Indian market with experience centres. Here’s a detailed look at the stories that shaped the week.

Renault Duster Achieves 5-Star BNCAP Rating

Renault’s Duster SUV has become the latest model to be crash-tested under India’s Bharat NCAP (BNCAP) programme, and it has emerged with a full five-star safety rating for adult occupant protection. The Duster is the first Renault vehicle to undergo BNCAP testing, and its score places it among the safest cars in its segment. The test results cover major safety parameters, including frontal offset, side impact, and pole side impact. While the Duster’s performance is commendable, it also raises the bar for competitors in the compact SUV space, where safety ratings are increasingly influencing buyer decisions.

Tesla Model Y L: Six-Seater Electric SUV Launched

Tesla has introduced a new variant of its popular Model Y, called the Model Y L. This version features a longer body and extended wheelbase, enabling three-row seating in a 2+2+2 layout. The Model Y L is equipped with a dual-motor all-wheel-drive system and offers a claimed range of over 500 km. This launch follows the earlier introduction of the Model Y 250 Plus, which delivers a range of over 750 km with a single motor and rear-wheel drive. The six-seater configuration targets families seeking an electric SUV with genuine third-row usability, a segment that is still nascent in India.

Mercedes-Benz CLA Electric: Entry-Level EV Sedan

Mercedes-Benz has launched the CLA Electric as its entry-level electric sedan in India. The CLA Electric is positioned below the EQS sedan and aims to attract luxury EV buyers with a more accessible price point. It features a sleek coupe-like design, a 10.1-inch touchscreen, and improved connected features. The CLA Electric is expected to compete with the BMW i4 and the upcoming Audi Q6 e-tron. Mercedes-Benz’s move underscores the growing demand for luxury electric vehicles in India, where charging infrastructure is steadily expanding.

Maruti Suzuki’s Four-Car Launch Plan by 2027

Maruti Suzuki is gearing up for one of its busiest launch periods in recent years. Over the next 12 to 18 months, the company plans to introduce four new cars: a Brezza facelift, the YMC EV, and updates to the Fronx and Baleno. The Brezza facelift is expected to feature a 10.1-inch touchscreen, replacing the current 9-inch unit, along with enhanced connected car technology. The YMC EV, a dedicated electric model, is slated for launch by late 2026 or early 2027 and will be offered with two battery pack options. The Fronx and Baleno updates will likely include minor styling tweaks and feature upgrades. This aggressive product pipeline signals Maruti’s intent to defend its market share against rising competition from Hyundai, Tata, and MG.

JSW Motors Enters India with Experience Centres

JSW Motors, the automotive arm of the JSW Group, has announced plans to set up at least four ‘experience centres’ in key Indian cities, including Mumbai, New Delhi, and Ahmedabad, before launching its debut car in the second half of 2026. The company is hoping to create a premium brand image by offering immersive customer experiences rather than traditional dealerships. JSW’s entry adds another player to India’s already crowded car market, but its focus on EVs and premium positioning could carve out a niche.

Honda to Exit South Korea Car Market; No Impact on India

Honda has confirmed it will exit the South Korean car market by the end of 2026, citing low sales volumes. The company will continue to provide after-sales service and focus on its motorcycle business in the country. For Indian consumers, this move has no direct implications—Honda Cars India remains a strong player with models like the City, Amaze, and Elevate. However, it reflects the broader global trend of automakers rationalising their operations in markets where they face stiff competition from local brands.

Analysis and Implications

The week’s news highlights several key trends. First, safety is becoming a decisive factor in India’s car market. The Duster’s five-star BNCAP rating will likely pressure other manufacturers to improve crash test performance, especially in the popular compact SUV segment. Second, the EV landscape is diversifying: Tesla’s six-seater Model Y L targets family buyers, while Mercedes’ CLA Electric opens luxury EV ownership to a wider audience. Third, Maruti’s four-car blitz shows that even the market leader cannot rest on its laurels—it must continuously refresh its lineup to stay relevant. Finally, new entrants like JSW Motors signal that India’s automotive growth story is attracting fresh investment.

Looking ahead, the Indian car market is set for an exciting phase. BNCAP will continue to influence vehicle design, with more models expected to be tested. EV adoption will accelerate as new models from Tesla, Mercedes, and Maruti hit the roads. Maruti’s YMC EV, in particular, could be a game-changer if priced competitively. Meanwhile, JSW Motors’ experience centre model may redefine how cars are sold in India. The next 12 to 18 months will be crucial in shaping the industry’s trajectory.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Bharat NCAP?

Bharat NCAP (New Car Assessment Programme) is India’s own crash test rating system that evaluates vehicle safety for adult and child occupants. Cars are rated from 1 to 5 stars based on performance in frontal offset, side impact, and pole side impact tests.

How much does the Tesla Model Y L cost in India?

Official pricing for the Tesla Model Y L in India has not been announced yet. It is expected to be priced higher than the standard Model Y, likely around ₹60-70 lakh (ex-showroom).

When will Maruti Suzuki launch the YMC EV?

The YMC EV is expected to launch by late 2026 or early 2027, with two battery pack options.

Will Honda’s exit from South Korea affect Indian customers?

No. Honda’s decision to exit South Korea is specific to that market. Honda Cars India continues to operate normally, and after-sales service in India remains unaffected.
