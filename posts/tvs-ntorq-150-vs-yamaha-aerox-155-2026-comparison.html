TVS Ntorq 150 vs Yamaha Aerox 155: Which Sporty Scooter Deserves Your Money in 2026?

The TVS Ntorq 150 is the stronger value pick for city riders who want sporty styling, connected features and easier daily usability. The Yamaha Aerox 155 is the better performance scooter, with more power, higher claimed mileage and a more premium maxi-scooter

The TVS Ntorq 150 is the sharper value pick for riders who want sporty styling, connected features, easier city use and a lower entry price. The Yamaha Aerox 155 is the stronger performance scooter, with more power, higher claimed efficiency and a more premium maxi-scooter feel. If your budget is tight and your riding is mostly urban, the Ntorq makes more sense. If you want the fastest, most motorcycle-like scooter experience, the Aerox still has the edge.

Quick Verdict

The TVS Ntorq 150 vs Yamaha Aerox 155 comparison is not just a numbers game. Both scooters target young riders who want something more exciting than a regular family scooter, but their personalities are different. The Ntorq 150 feels like the more accessible, feature-heavy and city-friendly choice. The Aerox 155 feels like the more premium, faster and more mature performance scooter.

The most important difference is price. The Ntorq 150 starts around Rs 1.11 lakh ex-showroom in Delhi, while the Aerox 155 starts around Rs 1.43 lakh. That gap is large enough to pay for riding gear, insurance differences, accessories, or several months of fuel. The Aerox justifies part of its premium with a liquid-cooled 155cc engine, more power, stronger top-end performance and a maxi-scooter design. The Ntorq fights back with better torque on paper, a lower kerb weight, lower seat height, higher ground clearance and a more aggressive value proposition.

Performance and Riding Feel

The Aerox 155 wins the pure performance round. Its 155cc liquid-cooled engine produces 15 PS, which gives it a more energetic top-end and better high-speed confidence. It is the scooter for riders who often use open roads, want stronger overtaking ability and enjoy a more committed sporty riding character. The Aerox also feels more premium because of the way its bodywork, engine and stance come together. It is not trying to be a simple family scooter with sporty stickers. It is built around a different scooter format.

The Ntorq 150 is not slow, though. Its 149.7cc engine makes 13.2 PS and 14.2 Nm, so it has a useful torque advantage on paper. That matters in city riding, where quick launches, low-speed response and short overtakes count more than top speed. The Ntorq is also lighter at 115 kg, which helps it feel easier to manage in traffic. Riders upgrading from a 110cc or 125cc scooter may find the Ntorq 150 more instantly friendly, while the Aerox may feel wider, heavier and more premium but less conventionally practical.

Daily Use and Ownership

Daily usability is where the comparison becomes more nuanced. The Aerox has a larger underseat storage area, and its 24.5-litre space is a real advantage if you want to carry a full-face helmet or a bigger bag. But the Aerox does not have the same flat-floor convenience that many Indian scooter buyers still value. Its central tunnel gives it a sporty maxi-scooter feel, but it also reduces the ability to place bags between your feet.

The Ntorq 150 has a more familiar scooter layout and friendlier dimensions. Its 770 mm seat height is easier for shorter riders than the Aerox's 790 mm seat. Its 155 mm ground clearance is also better for rough roads and speed breakers. The 5.8-litre fuel tank is slightly larger than the Aerox's 5.5 litres, although the Aerox's higher claimed mileage can offset that in range calculations.

Ownership costs will depend heavily on service pricing, tyre replacement, fuel economy, insurance and how hard the scooter is ridden. The Ntorq should appeal to buyers who want lower upfront cost and strong feature value. The Aerox will suit riders willing to pay more for performance, premium design and Yamaha's refined engine package.

Buying Recommendation

Buy the TVS Ntorq 150 if you want the better value package, ride mostly in the city, care about connected features, want easier handling, or need a sporty scooter that does not demand Aerox money. It is the more sensible choice for college riders, city commuters, first-time premium scooter buyers and anyone upgrading from a 125cc scooter.

Buy the Yamaha Aerox 155 if you want the fastest and most premium scooter in this pair. It is better for riders who enjoy performance, do longer open-road runs, want a maxi-scooter stance and are comfortable paying more for Yamaha's liquid-cooled 155cc engine package.

Final verdict: The Ntorq 150 is the smarter value buy for most urban riders in 2026. The Aerox 155 is the enthusiast's pick. If your decision is emotional and you want the scooter that feels more special at speed, choose the Aerox. If your decision is practical and budget-aware, choose the Ntorq.

FAQ

Which is more powerful, TVS Ntorq 150 or Yamaha Aerox 155?

The Yamaha Aerox 155 is more powerful, producing about 15 PS compared with the Ntorq 150's 13.2 PS.

Which scooter has better torque?

The TVS Ntorq 150 has the higher torque figure on paper, at 14.2 Nm compared with 13.9 Nm for the Aerox 155.

Which scooter gives better mileage?

The Yamaha Aerox 155 has the higher claimed mileage figure at about 45 km/l, while the Ntorq 150 is rated around 40 km/l.

Which scooter is better for daily city use?

The TVS Ntorq 150 is easier to recommend for daily city use because it is lighter, cheaper, has a lower seat height and offers better ground clearance.

Which scooter is better for performance riders?

The Yamaha Aerox 155 is better for performance-focused riders because of its stronger engine, higher top speed and more premium maxi-scooter character.
