Ford's Do-Not-Drive Recall Hits Bronco Sport and Maverick: What Owners Need to Know

By Car News Desk | June 3, 2026

Trending keywords: Ford recall, Bronco Sport, Maverick, do not drive, NHTSA.

Why it matters

Ford has issued an urgent do-not-drive advisory for 4,653 vehicles, covering certain 2021-2026 Bronco Sport SUVs and 2022-2026 Maverick pickups. Fox Business reported that the issue involves front lower control arm ball joints that may have been incorrectly installed or repaired at the factory.

Road & Track, citing NHTSA documents, said a control arm could disconnect from the front wheel knuckle if the ball joint fails. That is why affected owners are being told to park the vehicle until inspection and repair are complete.

The affected group is relatively small, but the warning is serious. Owners should check their VIN through Ford or NHTSA and contact a dealer before driving if their vehicle is included.

Quick hits

  • The recall affects 2,357 Mavericks and 2,296 Bronco Sports.
  • Ford is covering inspection and repair costs.
  • Some dealers can arrange towing or mobile inspections.

Sources: Fox Business and Road & Track
